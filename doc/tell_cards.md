# Tell Cards

A special feature are the Wilhelm Tell cards or Seasonal cards used in Austria, Czech Republic, Hungary, also known as Double German deck. The designation "Double German" refers to the fact that the upper and lower parts of the card are identical, thus allowing the cards not having to be turned upright during play to ensure readability.

The four card symbols (card suits) are Acorn, Leaf (Green), Heart, and Bells.

The Aces (Dausers) depict scenes of the seasons. Often in the following assignment and representation:

- *Heart* (Piros): young woman in spring scene, alternatively blooming landscape, lovers or festive scene
- *Leaf* (Zöld): woman with sickle in hand during summer scene bringing in hay
- *Acorn* (Makk): one or several women during autumn wine harvest or wine pressing, also hunting or forest scene possible
- *Bells* (Tök): old woman carrying firewood in a snow-covered winter landscape, alternatively sleigh, winter village scene

The main card motives on the twelve picture cards often depict protagonists from the story of Swiss national hero "Wilhelm Tell" by Friedrich Schiller as follows:

*Acorn (Makk)*:

- *King*: Wilhelm Tell
- *Upper*: Walter Fürst
- *Under*: Walter (Tell's son)

*Leaf/Green (Zöld)*:

- *King*: Werner Stauffacher
- *Upper*: Gertrud (Stauffacher’s wife)
- *Under*: Arnold von Melchtal

*Heart (Piros)*:

- *King*: Baron von Attinghausen
- *Upper*: Berta von Bruneck
- *Under*: Ulrich von Rudenz

*Bells (Tök)*:

- *King*: Hermann Gessler
- *Upper*: Rudolf (von) Harras
- *Under*: Frießhardt (Soldier)

The further number cards (6, 7, 8, 9, 10 and Aces) show the following typical motives:

- The Rütli Oath of the Confederates
- Stauffacher seeks counsel from Gertrud
- The shepherd, the fisherman and the hunter at the lake; Baumgarten flees
- Landenberg has the old Melchtal blinded
- Tell ferries Baumgarten across the stormy lake
- Gessler's hat on the pole in Altdorf; the guards at the hat (Frießhardt/Leuthold)
- The apple shot (Tell and his son Walter)
- Tell's storm journey; the leap at the Tell's cliff
- The Hollow Way: Death of Gessler
- Death of Attinghausen; Rudenz confesses to the people; liberation of the prisoners
- Berta among the country folk; destruction of the castles
- Tell’s encounter with Johann Parricida
- Hedwig (Tell's wife) repels pursuers

The exact assignment of the motives to each card varies by manufacturer and print.
