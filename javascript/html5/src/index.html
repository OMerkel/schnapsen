📖 How to Play — Schnapsen

Objective

Build card points through tricks and marriages, then win hands by correctly declaring 66 on lead or by winning the full-play finish.

Tricks and Talon

While the talon is open, any card may be played. Once it is exhausted or closed, strict follow-suit and Atout rules apply.

Open talon bonus action: The active leader may swap the open Atout with the lowest Atout from hand (24-card: 9, 20-card: Unter) while more than two cards remain in talon. This is not allowed after talon close.

Marriage scoring is explicit: announce the suit first, then lead König or Ober of that suit. If you skip the announcement, you score no marriage points.

Marriage points count only after the announcing player wins at least one trick in the hand: immediate if a trick is already won, otherwise deferred until the first won trick. If immediate marriage points bring the active leader to 66 or more, declare-66 is legal immediately after the announcement.

Seat layout is fixed: Player South remains at the south hand and Player North remains at the north hand. Only the active hand is shown face-up; inactive hand cards stay as backfaces.

All action buttons and card clicks target the currently active player.

Visual states: Open talon is shown crosswise over the open Atout card (partly visible). Closed talon keeps the stack visible with a face-down Atout above talon. Exhausted talon hides both talon and Atout stack.

Terminology: This app uses Atout as the preferred label for the suit with beating priority.

Win Condition

Declare-66 is a lead action: only the active leader may claim 66 before a trick card is played. A correct claim (66+) wins immediately.

A wrong claim loses immediately: opponent gets 2 game points, or 3 if claimant has won no trick (Schwarz). The same 2/3 penalty applies when a player closes talon and then fails to declare 66 before cards run out.

If nobody claims 66 and cards run out, the last trick winner wins the hand for exactly 1 game point, regardless of card-point totals.

Default match target is first to 7 Bummerl points. Alternate deck variants and player types are configurable in game settings.
